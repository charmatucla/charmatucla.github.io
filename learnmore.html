What is OCD?

OCD stands for obsessive-compulsive disorder, which is characterised by two main components: obsessions and compulsions

Obsessions: unwanted and intrusive thoughts, images, and urges

Compulsions: repetitive mental or physical actions a person feels compelled to do as a result of their obsession. These actions are done to either prevent an undesired situation or to prevent or reduce feelings of anxiety/distress.

Let's talk more about obsessions below:

Obsessions:

As mentioned before, obsessions are intrusive and unwanted thoughts, images, or urges. Because these are intrusive, this means that they are ego-dystonic and don't align with a person's values. For instance, someone with Harm OCD may have intrusive thoughts about harming people but these thoughts do not align with their values and the individual does not want to act on them. The individual doesn't have these intrusive thoughts because they want to have them but precisely because they don't.

Quick thought experiment: Try not to imagine a pink elephant

I'm sure that by the time you read the words pink elephant, you were already imagining one!

This is similar to what happens when people with OCD experience intrusive thoughts. The more they try not to think of something, the more it comes into their mind. People with out OCD experience intrusive thoughts as well on occassion. The difference is that for people with OCD, these thoughts become "sticky". These thoughts are persistent and distressing, causing the individual to perform compulsions to alleviate or prevent feelings of distress.
